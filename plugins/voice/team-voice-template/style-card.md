This file is the system-prompt-equivalent content the `/write-as` skill loads to draft text in your voice. Hand-author it from your own writing samples (see `corpus.md` if you keep one — 15–20 raw human-written samples is a typical baseline).

A good style card answers, in your own voice rules:

## Voice fingerprint

- What sentence rhythms do you use? (e.g., terminal fragments, short bursts, long winding clauses)
- Punctuation tics. (e.g., hyphens-not-em-dashes, scare quotes on jargon, parenthetical hedges)
- Vocabulary register. (e.g., anglo-saxon over latinate, technical without being academic)
- Casual tics. (e.g., `idk`, `imo`, `gotta`, lowercase abbreviations, ALL CAPS on one load-bearing word)
- Anti-slop list — phrases you NEVER use. (e.g., "I hope this email finds you well", "circling back", "synergy")

## Notes on what makes a good corpus sample

- Pure human-written text (no AI-assisted drafts)
- A mix of registers: email, X, LinkedIn, longform, telegraphic notes
- Recent enough to reflect current voice (older samples drift)
